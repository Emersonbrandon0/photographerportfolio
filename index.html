A Little About Me

My own curiosity for different ways of living have brought me to many new and unfamiliar territories-from areas as far away as the holy lands of Varanasi-a trip that I first took as a teenager, which served as the catalyst for the self-titled film and foundation for my ongoing Holy Men series-to culturally-distinct neighborhoods tucked away between the graffitied-streets of Brooklyn-as depicted by my annual pilgrimage to document Halloween in Brooklyn-a favorite personal project. A firm believer in fostering creativity and fulfillment through personal pursuits, I have written and directed my first scripted film entitled People of the Delta. The film not only featured members of the Daasanach and Hamar groups of Southern Ethiopia as the actors, but also collaborated with many of them for creative input throughout the entire film.

Most recently, I traveled twice to the Kurdish regions of Iraq and Syria to document the volunteer fighters of the PKK and YPG. The new series, Guerrilla Fighters of Kurdistan, highlight the men and women on the frontline of the ongoing conflict with ISIS-a war that is headlined daily but now humanized through my enlightening portraits. The project was featured in Vanity Fair: Italia, Internazionale and ran as The Independent's Saturday Magazine cover story.

Many times pictures can speak to our souls, from a cute picture of a puppy to a expose piece telling the horrors of forced child labor. Sometimes these images stick with us, other times they fade away, to the place where we put things we don't want to remember. But sometimes there are things we want to make sure we remember.
